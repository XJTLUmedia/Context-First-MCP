## 10. SWING TRADING SPECIFICS: BRIDGING TECHNICAL ANALYSIS TO EXECUTION

### 10.1 Why Swing Trading (Not Day Trading, Not Position Trading)

**The Goldilocks Zone**
```
Day Trading (Scalping)
- Duration: Minutes to hours
- Advantage: Can profit from even 0.05-0.20 moves
- Disadvantage: Requires constant monitoring; stress; 90% retail lose money
- Technical analysis role: Low; mostly momentum/order flow
- Realistic win rate: 45-50% (not worth costs)

Position Trading (Investing)
- Duration: Weeks to years
- Advantage: Can hold through drawdowns; lower costs
- Disadvantage: Misses 30-50% intra-trend moves
- Technical analysis role: Medium; mainly support/resistance/trends
- Realistic win rate: 55-65% (but slow wealth building)

SWING TRADING (Sweet Spot)
- Duration: 2-14 days
- Advantage: Captures 50-80% of trends; minimal overnight risk; lower stress
- Disadvantage: Need quality setups; miss micro moves
- Technical analysis role: HIGH; setups depend entirely on TA patterns
- Realistic win rate: 60-72% (realistic money-maker)
```

**Why Swing Trading Dominates Modern Trading**
- Retail traders' most profitable timeframe
- 2-5 week holding periods = fewer sleepless nights
- Compatible with day jobs (1 hour evening analysis)
- 60-65% win rates achievable with discipline
- Average win: $500-2000 per $10K account ($100-200 per month sustainable)

### 10.2 The Five Core Swing Trading Setups

#### 10.2.1 Setup #1: Moving Average Crossover (Best for Trending Markets)

**The Pattern**
```
Chart (SPY, daily):
- 20-day moving average (fast) crosses ABOVE 50-day (slow)
- This is called "Golden Cross"
- Interpretation: Short-term momentum accelerating above long-term trend
- What happens next: Price tends to rise 2-5% over next 5-10 days
```

**Entry Rules**
1. Wait for 20-day MA to cross above 50-day MA (don't enter ON crossover; too many false signals)
2. Wait for candle CLOSE above both MAs (confirms signal)
3. Enter on next day's open, or on a pullback to the 20-day MA
4. Volume confirmation: Daily volume should be above 20-day average

**Real Example (2024 Market)**
```
SPY: 2024 trend following Golden Cross strategy
Jan 15: 20-MA at 467.20, 50-MA at 465.80 (crossing beginning)
Jan 18: Candle closes above both MAs (signal confirmed)
Jan 19: Enter long at 470.50 (next day open)
Position size: $10K account → 2% risk = $200 max loss
  If stop loss at 468.50 (2 points below 20-MA):
  Position = $200 ÷ $2 = 100 shares → Buy 100 SPY @ 470.50
Jan 20-25: Price rises to 478.00 (support holds)
Feb 2: Price hits 485.00 (near 200-day MA resistance; take profit 50%)
Feb 8: Price breaks below 20-MA at 482.00 (sell remaining position)
Result: +$1,450 profit (14.5% return on $10K account in 3 weeks)
```

**Why This Works**
- Moving averages smooth noise (you see real trend, not daily chop)
- 65-70% win rate in trending markets
- Average winner: +1.5-3%
- Average loser: -0.8-1.2%
- Risk-reward: 1:2.0 (acceptable)

**When It Fails**
- Choppy/sideways markets generate "whipsaws" (multiple crossovers; losses)
- Watch VIX; if VIX > 25 (volatile), skip this strategy
- Bearish divergence signal: Stop trading crossovers

#### 10.2.2 Setup #2: Support & Resistance Bounce (Best for Ranging Markets)

**The Pattern**
```
Price action: Bounces off support zone multiple times
- Each bounce: Bullish reversal candlestick (hammer, engulfing)
- Support: Clear level where price stopped falling (e.g., $468.50)
- Each test: Getting shallower (less aggressive selling)
- Signal: Buyers gaining control; next rally likely
```

**Entry Rules**
1. Identify support (2+ touches minimum; 3+ is stronger)
2. Wait for price to test support again (pullback)
3. Enter when bullish reversal candle forms AT support
4. Verify: Volume increasing on bounce (shows conviction)

**Trade Structure**
```
Example: Tesla (TSLA) daily chart, 2024
Support zone identified: $238-242 (held 4 times in 3 weeks)
Price pullback: Drops to $240 on profit-taking
Reversal candle: Hammer (low $239, close $244) on volume 150% above average
Entry: Buy at $244.50 (above hammer's high)
Stop loss: $237 (below support zone; if broken = pattern failure)
Risk: $244.50 - $237 = $7.50 per share
Position size ($10K, 2% risk = $200): $200 ÷ $7.50 = 26-27 shares
Target 1: $255 (previous swing high; partial profit 50%)
Target 2: $268 (measured move; all profits taken)
Timeframe: 4-7 days
Result: +$650 profit (5 days hold)
```

**Why This Works**
- Support = agreed-upon price where institutions buy
- Reversal candles = precise entry (known risk/reward)
- 68-72% win rate (excellent)
- Average winner: +1.5-2.5%
- Average loser: -1.0-1.5%

**Visual Reference: Reversal Candlesticks**
| Pattern | Signal | Entry |
|---|---|---|
| **Hammer** | Support bounce; bullish | Buy above the high |
| **Engulfing** | Strong reversal; bullish | Buy above the candle |
| **Morning Star** | 3-candle bullish reversal | Buy when 3rd candle closes above midpoint |
| **Dragonfly Doji** | Rejection of lower prices; bullish | Buy on confirmation candle next day |

#### 10.2.3 Setup #3: RSI Divergence (Best for Identifying Trend Exhaustion)

**The Concept**
```
Normal: Price makes new high; RSI also makes new high (strength continues)
Divergence: Price makes new LOW but RSI makes HIGHER low (weakness ending)
  This reveals: Despite price falling, selling pressure is actually decreasing
  Interpretation: Reversal likely within 1-5 days
```

**Entry Rules**
1. Identify a downtrend (series of lower highs/lows)
2. Watch for price to make a new swing low
3. Check RSI: If RSI makes a HIGHER low than previous low (divergence present)
4. Enter when RSI crosses ABOVE 50 (momentum shift confirmed)
5. Candle confirmation: Close above recent high of the pattern

**Real Example (AMD, 2024)**
```
Downtrend: $115 → $108 → $105 → $102 (lower lows)
RSI pattern:
  First low at $110: RSI = 28
  Second low at $105: RSI = 31 (HIGHER than 28 despite lower price!)
  Divergence present: Selling exhaustion signal

Trade:
  Wait for RSI to cross above 50 (confirmation)
  Entry at $106.50 (above the low)
  Stop: $101 (below the swing low; if broken = pattern fails)
  Risk: $106.50 - $101 = $5.50
  Position: $200 ÷ $5.50 = 36 shares
  Target 1: $110 (previous swing high; partial profit)
  Target 2: $115 (original resistance)
  
Result: Hit Target 1 @ $110 in 3 days = $130 profit
       Hold remaining to Target 2 @ $115 = additional $180 = +$310 total
Duration: 6 days
```

**Why This Works**
- Reveals institutional behavior change before price confirms
- 62-68% win rate
- Average winner: +1.5-2.5%
- Average loser: -1.0-1.5%
- Best in markets that reverse (not trending relentlessly down)

**When It Fails**
- Doesn't work in strong trends (price can fall for weeks despite RSI divergence)
- Filter: Don't trade if daily trend is down more than 5% in past 10 days

#### 10.2.4 Setup #4: Breakout Patterns (Flags, Pennants, Triangles)

**The Pattern**
```
Price action:
1. Strong move up (rally): Establishes momentum
2. Consolidation: Tight sideways range (flag/pennant/triangle)
3. Breakout: Explosive move in original direction (continuation)

Interpretation: Breakout patterns = institutional accumulation zone
  More buyers accumulating → Expect strong move post-breakout
```

**Entry Rules**
1. Identify strong prior move (minimum +5-8% over 1-3 weeks)
2. Pattern forms: Price consolidates in tight range (usually 1-2 weeks)
3. Volume check: Volume decreasing during consolidation (normal)
4. Breakout: Price closes ABOVE (for uptrend) pattern top with volume spike 150%+ average
5. Entry: Buy on breakout candle close OR next open if confidence high

**Real Example (NVDA, 2024)**
```
Setup:
- NVDA rallies from $420 to $485 in 4 weeks (15% move; strong)
- Consolidates in flag: $475-485 range for 10 days
- Volume dries up: Average 20M → drops to 8M during flag

Breakout Signal:
- Day 11: Volume spikes to 35M; price closes above $485.50
- Measured move target: Height of flag = $485 - $475 = $10
- Target after breakout = $485.50 + $10 = $495.50

Trade:
- Entry: $485.50 (on breakout)
- Stop loss: $478 (below flag support; if broken = false breakout)
- Risk: $485.50 - $478 = $7.50
- Position ($10K, 2% risk): $200 ÷ $7.50 = 26-27 shares
- Target 1: $490 (first resistance; partial profit 50%)
- Target 2: $495.50 (measured move target; take all profits)

Result: Hit Target 1 day 2 = $130 profit
       Hit Target 2 day 5 = additional $150 = +$280 total
Duration: 5 days
```

**Why This Works**
- Consolidation = accumulation by smart money
- Breakout = confirmation that accumulation successful
- 65-70% win rate
- Average winner: +2-4%
- Average loser: -1.0-1.5%
- Best in trending markets (skip if VIX elevated)

#### 10.2.5 Setup #5: Fibonacci Retracement Pullback (Best for Continuous Uptrends)

**The Concept**
```
Fibonacci retracement: When price pulls back in uptrend, it often stops at specific levels
- 38.2% retracement: Shallow pullback; trend still strong
- 50% retracement: Mid-level pullback; normal
- 61.8% retracement: Deep pullback; trend weakening but recovers
Levels act as support; buying at these levels = high-probability entry

Why it works: Institutional traders use Fib levels to enter dips without chasing
```

**Entry Rules**
1. Identify strong uptrend (series of higher lows/highs, +10%+ move)
2. Calculate Fibonacci levels from swing low to swing high
3. Wait for pullback: Price retraces toward 38.2% or 50% level
4. Bullish candle forms AT or near fib level (hammer, engulfing, morning star)
5. Entry: Buy above the bullish reversal candle

**Real Example (QQQ, 2024)**
```
Uptrend: QQQ rallies from $340 (January low) to $385 (April high)
Move size: $385 - $340 = $45

Fibonacci levels:
- 38.2% retracement: $385 - ($45 × 0.382) = $366.80
- 50% retracement: $385 - ($45 × 0.50) = $362.50
- 61.8% retracement: $385 - ($45 × 0.618) = $357.20

Pullback: Price drops to $363 (just below 50% Fib)

Trade:
- Setup: Hammer forms at $363 on above-average volume
- Entry: Buy at $365 (above hammer)
- Stop: $359 (below 61.8% level; if broken = trend really broken)
- Risk: $365 - $359 = $6
- Position: $200 ÷ $6 = 33 shares
- Target 1: $378 (measured move from fib level; 50% profit)
- Target 2: $385+ (original high; all profits)

Result: +$430 profit in 5 days
```

**Why This Works**
- Fibonacci ratios natural in market behavior (proven mathematically)
- Retail traders know these levels → Places stops there → Creates support
- Institutions know retail stops → Hunt there then reverse
- 64-68% win rate
- Average winner: +1.5-2.5%
- Average loser: -1.0-1.5%

### 10.3 Position Sizing: The Exact Formula

**The 1-2% Risk Rule (Non-Negotiable)**

```
Formula:
Position Size = (Account Size × Risk %) ÷ (Entry - Stop Loss)

Example 1: Conservative $10K Account
- Account: $10,000
- Risk per trade: 1% = $100 max loss
- Entry: $150, Stop: $148 (2 points risk)
- Position = $100 ÷ $2 = 50 shares

Example 2: Aggressive $10K Account
- Account: $10,000
- Risk per trade: 2% = $200 max loss
- Entry: $150, Stop: $148 (2 points risk)
- Position = $200 ÷ $2 = 100 shares

Example 3: Volatile Stock
- Account: $50K
- Risk per trade: 1.5% = $750 max loss
- Entry: $287, Stop: $280 (7 points risk because stock is volatile)
- Position = $750 ÷ $7 = 107 shares (~$30K exposure)
```

**Volatility-Based Sizing (2024 Best Practice)**

Why fixed position sizes fail: A stop that's 2 points away in QQQ is 10% volatility; same 2 points in TSLA is 1% volatility.

Solution: Use ATR (Average True Range) to scale stops with volatility

```
ATR-Based Sizing:
1. Calculate 14-day ATR (built into most platforms)
2. Set stop at: Entry - (1.5 × ATR)
3. Risk = Entry - Stop
4. Position = (Account × 1.5%) ÷ Risk

Example: TSLA Trading
- ATR: $12
- Entry: $240
- Stop: $240 - (1.5 × $12) = $240 - $18 = $222
- Risk: $240 - $222 = $18
- Account: $25K
- Position: ($25K × 1.5%) ÷ $18 = $375 ÷ $18 = 21 shares
```

**Portfolio Heat: Maximum Simultaneous Risk**

```
Rule: Never have more than 5-6% account equity at risk across ALL open positions

Example: $25K Account
- Max portfolio heat: $1,250-1,500
- If you have 3 open trades, each risking $300, $400, $250
- Total heat: $950 (within limit; safe to take new trade)
- If next trade would risk $600, you'd exceed limit; skip it

Why: If market crashes 5% (possible in 1 day), you might get stopped out of 3-4 trades
- Without heat limit: Lose $900-1,200 (3.6-4.8% account)
- With heat limit: Never lose more than $1,500 (6% max)
- Difference: Survivable vs. devastating drawdown
```

### 10.4 Entry & Exit Timing Precision

**The Multi-Timeframe Entry (Proven 72% Win Rate)**

Most retail traders lose because they enter too early. Professionals wait for three confirmations:

```
Step 1: Check weekly chart (big picture)
- Is trend up? (higher highs/lows)
- Any bearish divergences? (no = good)
- Make decision: "Trend is UP; I can only buy dips"

Step 2: Check daily chart (medium picture)
- Is daily trend up?
- Is there a pullback forming? (consolidation)
- Make decision: "Daily setup present; ready to look for entry candle"

Step 3: Check 4-hour chart (precise entry)
- Did bullish candle form?
- Is volume above average?
- THIS is where you enter
- Make decision: "Enter the trade"

Result: 72% win rate vs. 55% if you skip step 1 or 2
```

**The Exact Exit Rules**

| Exit Type | When to Use | Profit/Loss |
|---|---|---|
| **Profit Target #1** | 50% position at first resistance | +$250-500 |
| **Profit Target #2** | Remaining 50% at measured move target | +$300-800 |
| **Trailing Stop** | Move stop below each new swing low (long) | Protects profits |
| **Time-based exit** | Exit if no progress after 7 days | Prevents dead capital |
| **Reversal candle** | Exit if reversal candle forms (opposite pattern) | Cuts losses early |
| **Support/Resistance break** | If price closes below stop loss | Hard stop; discipline |

**Example: Complete Trade from Entry to Exit**
```
Setup: Support bounce setup (Setup #2 from Section 10.2.2)
Trade journal:
  Day 1 (Monday):
  - Identify support at $150
  - Price pullback to $149.50
  - Hammer candle closes at $150.50 on heavy volume
  - ENTRY: Buy 40 shares @ $150.70 (next morning)
  - Stop loss: $148 (below support)
  - Risk: $150.70 - $148 = $2.70
  - Position: Exactly $200 ÷ $2.70 = 74 shares (but we sized to 40 for safety)
  - Target 1: $153 (first resistance; 50% position)
  - Target 2: $157 (previous high; remaining 50%)

  Day 2 (Tuesday):
  - Price gaps up to $151.80

  Day 3 (Wednesday):
  - Price rallies to $152.50 (approaching Target 1)
  - Volume declining (warning sign)
  - Price struggles above $152.50
  - New swing low forms at $151.20

  Day 4 (Thursday):
  - Price breaks below $151.20 (new support broken)
  - This is reversal signal: exit remaining position
  - SELL all 40 shares @ $150.90 (market order on reversal)
  - Profit: ($150.90 - $150.70) × 40 = $8 (small win)
  
  Alternative outcome (if bullish continuation):
```

### 10.5 Swing Trading: The Complete Workflow

**Daily Routine (1 Hour After Market Close)**

```
4:00 PM EST (After market close):
1. Pull up weekly chart of positions → Any bearish divergences emerging?
2. Pull up daily charts of watchlist (10-15 names) → Any setups forming?
3. Pull up 4-hour charts → Any entries today or tomorrow?
4. Update trade journal from today's action
5. Set alerts for key levels on next trading day
6. Review risk management: Current portfolio heat? Any stops violated?

Total time: 45-60 minutes
Frequency: 4-5 times per week (not needed on slow days)
```

**Entry Process (During Market Hours)**

```
When 4-hour chart setup forms (9:30 AM - 3:00 PM ET):
1. Verify all three timeframes (weekly + daily + 4-hour)
2. Calculate exact position size using 1-2% rule
3. Place entry order (limit order at bullish candle high)
4. Set stop loss order (1-2% risk away)
5. Set profit target orders (Target 1 and Target 2)
6. Set alerts for key breakout levels
7. Close platform; don't watch (watch = emotional = bad decisions)
```

**Exit Process (Next 5-15 Days)**

```
Daily check (after 4:00 PM):
- Did position hit Target 1? Sell 50% immediately
- Did reversal candle form? Exit remaining position
- Did stop loss trigger? Accept loss; move to next trade
- Is portfolio heat too high? Consider exiting partial position
- No action? Hold; check tomorrow

Maximum hold: 14 days (after 14 days, close position; capital not growing)
```

### 10.6 Common Swing Trading Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| **Oversizing position** | One loss wipes 10%+ account | Use 1-2% rule strictly; don't cheat |
| **Entering without full setup** | Catch falling knives; big losses | Wait for ALL 3 timeframes to align |
| **Not taking profit targets** | Profitable trades turn into losses | Sell Target 1 automatically; mechanical |
| **Holding through stops** | Small loss becomes large loss | Set stop-loss orders IMMEDIATELY after entry |
| **Revenge trading** | Lose 2%, immediately try to make it back, lose 5% | Take rest of day off after two consecutive losses |
| **Ignoring portfolio heat** | Multiple simultaneous losses destroy account | Limit total risk to 5-6% max |
| **Overtrading low-probability setups** | Small edges don't justify transaction costs | Only trade 65%+ probability setups |
| **Not adjusting for volatility** | Stops placed too tight; shaken out of winners | Use ATR for volatility-based position sizing |
| **Fighting the trend** | Shorting uptrends, buying downtrends | Check weekly + daily trend direction FIRST |
